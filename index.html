Small steps make big changes!

Timing is everything, or so they say. I think of a close friend of mine who decided that his time was now. Despite being at the start of the biggest period of uncertainty we had known for years, he had become convinced that if he didn’t act now, he would sink ever deeper into the quicksand of comfortability otherwise known as a steady career.

How do you know you will find work? You’ve already got a decent job, why start again? And, It seems too risky given the recession and the effect it has already had on the economy, were just a few of the questions he faced from friends and loved ones as he became ever more firm in his conviction. His answers were always the same i.e. because i’m not happy, because there’s only so much time left, because if i want to feel fulfilled, if i want it to change i’ve got no choice.

Looking back, I think it's clear to see that there was a man at the end of his tether. Stuck in a manual role that although was well paid, gave him little in the way of satisfaction. He would tell me that he wanted more. More than he could ever get in his current role. He wanted satisfaction, he wanted to go to work excited about the challenges of the day ahead. That if it meant a drop in earnings while he trained for a new role, he was convinced that in the long run, he would look back at this decision as the most important and positive one he's made.

That friend went by the name of Tony and I’ve known him all my life. Sometimes I listened to his advice, sometimes I didn't, but every time I thought he was wrong, I've lived to regret not following him. He has matured over the years, starting out as an unrealistic risk taker, an all night raver, a drunken dancer, into what i now feel is a much more grounded, reasonable and trustworthy companion. It took time, mistakes (more than you can imagine) and many regrets to get him to this point, but if anything, these mistakes and struggles were what made him who he is.

That friend is my inner voice and this time I'm following him. Standing at the start of a journey that hopefully, with enough hardwork and dedication, will lead to us to not just a more fulfilling career, but a more fulfilling life. I know nothing is certain, it could end up a mistake, but if there's even the slightest chance that it could work out, I'm in.

So on that note, welcome to our first ever blog. I invite you to follow me on this journey through the good times and the bad, as we take our first footsteps into something that might just inspire you to do the same. To follow your head and your heart, and make a change worth fighting for.
